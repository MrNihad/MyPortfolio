Suitability analysis for beekeeping in Estonia

Background

Bees have vital importance in our lives, not just in honey production as well as being the primary pollinator in nature. Therefore deciding where to put the beehives is very important. Several factors influence the bees, such as closeness to water sources, flora(land cover), and proximity to urban areas and agricultural fields. It seems like it would be a great idea to put it near agricultural fields because of pollination. However, one particular factor has lethal effects on bees, which is pesticides. So agricultural fields are not ideal locations for bees.

Aim

This research aims to find the best locations for beehives in Estonia using multi-criteria analysis. Especially for honey and wax production. This will help future beekeepers to choose the best place for the colony.

Data and Methods

For this research, five main factors were chosen. These are proximity to roads, urban areas, water sources, land cover type, and aspects. Each of these has a different effect on beekeeping. For example, beekeepers tend to avoid urban areas and choose a location far from roads because of greenhouse gas. On the other hand, closeness to water bodies and land cover type significantly impacts bees. Most of the datasets are from the Estonian Land Board, and only the land cover data is Copernicus Corine land cover data. As a methodology, the vector layers of roads, water bodies, urban areas, and land cover turned into raster data. Before turning land cover data to raster, the classification steps were done. I classified it into four categories based on land type. After rasterization, the proximity analysis is done using the proximity (raster distance) tool for roads, urban areas, and water bodies. Aspect data was created using elevation data. Then all these four datasets are reclassified into four categories using reclassify by table tool. Based on referenced articles, the multi-criteria decision matrix was created. The criteria index was 0.06. Table 1 illustrates the multi-criteria decision matrix. The main weights put on land cover type and closeness to water bodies. Then the final map was created using a raster calculator. Each layer is multiplied by its weight, and these raster layers are added together. As a final step, a polygonize tool is used to turn this final raster layer into a vector layer, and statistics by categories are used to calculate each suitable or unsuitable area’s sum.

Results

Results showed that the most suitable areas are located around water bodies and in forest cover types. The criteria for proximity to water bodies were taken from Mohd Noor Maris, N. (2008), and 0-200m was for highly suitable criteria. That is why the highly suitable areas are smaller than others. Also, the proximity to the road network has influenced it a lot. After area calculation, results showed that 52% of the study area is moderately suitable, 40% is less suitable, almost 2% highly suitable, and 5.6% is not suitable. Since Estonia has many forest areas, more than half of the study area is suitable for beekeeping. Figure 1 and Table 2 illustrate these results

Conclusion

The suitable areas for beekeeping in Estonia are analyzed using a multi-criteria matrix. Based on the analysis of five factors, more than half of the study area is suitable for beekeeping. However, the highly suitable areas contain a small portion of the study area. For future research, the analysis can be conducted on more datasets and additional criteria.
